Mission Statement

The mission of the American Cancer Society is to improve the lives of people with cancer and their families through advocacy, research, and patient support, to ensure everyone has an opportunity to prevent, detect, treat, and survive cancer.

About Us

The American Cancer Society, founded in 1913 in New York City by 10 doctors and five laypeople, was originally called the American Society for the Control of Cancer (ASCC). At the time, cancer was a taboo subject, often hidden by both doctors and patients. The founders aimed to raise public awareness through articles, bulletins, and educational outreach. In 1928, the iconic Sword of Hope symbol was introduced, representing the crusade against cancer and the medical mission of the society. Over the years, the logo has evolved but continues to embody the organization's dedication to saving lives and improving the quality of life through its united network of workers, caregivers, and volunteers.
